## 0. FOLLOW-UP CORRECTION (2026-06-28) — the Stage-2/3 re-run was ALSO confounded
The §4 "needs a Stage-2/3-ON probe" was attempted (the `gov{1,FULL}_cond{OFF,CHAOS}` grid) but used
**`stamina_ceiling=25`**, which relieves "toward NATURAL density" — i.e. it pulls density DOWN, directly

conditioning is **50** ("gentlest tasteful edit, stays Hard"; `governor_release_region.md` L18/L46). So that
grid is NOT a clean interaction read either; don't cite its by-ear result. **Durable, generalizable finding:
the right `stamina_ceiling` co-varies with the conditioning's density target** (25 tasteful on a plain chart,
too aggressive on density-cranked conditioning) — a concrete instance of the "weights slide with conditioning"
hypothesis. UI default fixed to 50. The pattern_temperature win (§3 / 06-27) is unaffected and stands.

## 1. The governor was running Stage 1 ONLY (the "felt disengaged" fact)

`export_typed_samples.py` defaults (verified in the script): `fatigue_penalty=2.0` (**Stage 1 ON**),
`stamina_ceiling=None` (**Stage 2 OFF**), `stamina_breathe=0.0` (**Stage 3 OFF**, and it needs
`stamina_ceiling` anyway). The grid passed none of the stamina/breathe flags. So "governor ON
throughout" in the SETUP entry meant **per-note fatigue/jack model only** — the per-region density
**stamina relief valve and the breathing arc were genuinely OFF**. The user's report ("it really does
feel like the stamina system & breathing was disengaged for these sets") is literally what the config
did, not a perception artifact. See `[[fatigue-governor]]` memory: release default is fatigue-only,
stamina/breathe **opt-in**.

## 2. Charts end early — song-specific tail truncation, INVARIANT to temp & conditioning

Last-note time (beats→sec via chart BPM) vs audio duration (librosa), `T0.7_condOFF`:

| song | chart ends | audio | coverage | gap |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **japa1** (突撃ガラスのニーソ姫) | 78s | 115s | **68%** | **37s** |
| **High School Love** | 94s | 115s | **82%** | 21s |
| OH WORLD | 109s | 110s | 99% | 1s |
| Deja loin | 127s | 127s | 100% | 0s |

The last-note time is **identical (±1s) across all 8 played settings** of each song (every temp ×
every cond mode), so the truncation is **not** a governor or conditioning effect — it is upstream.
It is **song-specific** and tracks outro structure: japa1 and HSL have quiet/sparse/held outros (HSL =
"calm long hold during much of the piano solo"); OH WORLD and Deja end busy → full coverage. Matches
the user's reports exactly (japa1 "really early", HSL "early", OH WORLD/Deja not flagged).

**Attribution:** this is **H5** (structural density fade at song end) compounded by the **onset head**
under-placing notes in quiet/melodic outros — the SAME melodic-under-placement measured in Probe 3B
(`jack_heaviness_findings.md`) and the H-arc-energy finding. Crucially breathing was **OFF** here, so
this is the **base** behavior, not the breathing-ceiling-collapse bug (H-arc-end, which only compounds
it when Stage 3 is on). → squarely a job for the **onset-head thread** (`HANDOFF.md` §3,
`sequence_aware_onset_plan.md`), now with a concrete symptom: tail truncation = onset silence in quiet
outros.

## 3. Note counts: temperature is a SHAPE lever, conditioning is the DENSITY lever

Total notes (sum over the chart):

| song | OFF (any T) | CHAOS (any T) | CHAOS Δ |
|---|---|---|---|
| Deja loin | ~377 | ~549 | **+46%** |
| OH WORLD | ~356 | ~462 | **+30%** |
| japa1 | ~374 | ~403 | +8% |
| High School Love | ~470 | ~462 | ~0% |

- **`pattern_temperature` barely moves note COUNT** (japa1 OFF: 375/375/373 at T0.7/1.0/1.2). It changes
  *which* patterns (jacks→jumps, Probe 1), not how many notes — a clean confirmation that temp is a
  pattern-shape axis, orthogonal to density. (condON sits between OFF and CHAOS, ~equal to OFF here.)
- **CHAOS conditioning cranks density most on the CALMEST songs** (Deja +46%, OH WORLD +30%) and least
  on the already-spicy ones (japa1 +8%, HSL flat). That is the chaos target being **out-of-distribution
  for linear songs** — the model is forced to add ~half-again as many notes to a regular song, which is
  exactly the "trying to meet the chaotic expectation and it just couldn't" awkwardness (Deja loin),
  quantified. Song-fit gating (SETUP note) reduces but does not remove this.

## 4. Method notes / caveats
- Coverage uses last note with a tap/hold-head/hold-tail token (`1`/`2`/`4`); BPM constant per song
  (single `#BPMS`), `#OFFSET` applied. ffprobe absent → durations via librosa in the conda env.
- This grid ran fatigue-only; do NOT read the flatness as a governor *defect* — it is governor *absence*
  (Stages 2–3). The conditioning×governor calibration question (does fixed `fatigue_penalty=2` mis-bite
  when conditioning shifts the logit distribution?) is UNTESTED here and needs a Stage-2/3-ON probe.
